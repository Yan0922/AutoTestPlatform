and benefit from them forever Now  lets dive into the first principle of studying business  Dont treat business research like studying classical physics This is a common misconception Ive noticed in my interactions with readers  Let me give you an example Take Disneys acquisition of Pixar  ( recommended reading  Pixar  A Match Made in Heaven with Disney )   Initially  the CEO of Disney  Michael Eisner  was in charge of the acquisition  However  he was so unpopular that Steve Jobs disliked him intensely  causing the collaboration between Disney and Pixar to stall  Later  Bob Iger  who was widely admired and liked by Jobs  became Disneys new CEO and quickly pushed the acquisition through  This acquisition was monumentalit paved the way for Disneys subsequent acquisitions of Marvel  Lucasfilm  and more  It essentially set Disneys moneymaking machine in motion  making it a brilliant business decision A student once asked  Aside from the human factor  could you list all the reasons why the initial negotiations failed and why they later succeeded  What specific changes led to this outcome  and how can we extract business rules from this to apply in the future I think this is a great attitude toward learning  However  theres a misconception here  this approach essentially treats business like physics  To understand this  we need to first clarify what we mean by physics The process of studying physics in human history generally goes like this Observation of the objective world  For example  an apple falls on someones head  Often  such observations are repeated  and its precisely because they recur that we pay attention to them Summarizing laws  Based on observations  we try to describe phenomena using various methods  For instance  the law of universal gravitation was derived from such observations  These laws are often expressed mathematically Verification  A key aspect of the scientific spirit is empirical validation  In short  anyone can propose explanations or laws about the world  but their accuracy must be tested through practice So  after Newton proposed the law of universal gravitation  physicists conducted experiments to verify its validity  Scientists worldwide could participate in this verification  Only when the results were consistently reproduced could the law be accepted  If new observations contradicted existing laws  scientists would propose new theories  like relativity  and repeat the verification process Now  let me ask you a question  Why do we study science or business  This is a broad question  but I think one thing is certain  we study science or business to guide our lives and work We study aerodynamics and fluid mechanics to build airplanes  cars  and ships  We study chemistry to create safer and more useful plastics  making life more convenient  So  why study business  Beyond satisfying our curiosity  we hope to observe phenomena  learn patterns  understand the world we live in  and ultimately apply this knowledge However  while both science and business guide our lives and work  they differ fundamentally  The process of studying physics is  observe the world  propose hypotheses  verify hypotheses  This process relies on two foundational logics  strict causality and reproducibility of laws What is strict causality  It means that everything happens for a reason; causes lead to effects  If an apple exists and you throw it into the air  it will fall to the ground  If the apple doesnt exist or you dont throw it  the result  ( the apple falling )  wont occur In other words  if you replicate all the initial conditions and execute the process  the result is inevitablea seamless progression from initial conditions to final outcome  Im sure you understand this What is reproducibility of laws  Simply put  if anyone  anywhere on Earth  throws an apple into the air  it will fall to the ground  This result is reproducible  Whether Newton did it centuries ago  the U S  President does it in the White House  or I do it  the outcome is the same  In other words  a law is a law because it can predict the future So  once you have the law of universal gravitation  you can confidently say that if you throw an apple upward  it will fall back down  The result is certain These two logics are so fundamental to our daily lives that few people bother to explain them  You can imagine that without strict causality and reproducibility  the entire edifice of science would collapse  If a law isnt reproducible  its not a law Now  back to the main topic  business Ive always said that the first principle of studying business is not to treat it like physics  What I mean is that when we study business  analyze companies  or summarize business theories  we must remain aware that the two pillars of sciencestrict causality and reproducibilitydont fully apply in the business world In other words  we mustnt treat business as a science or approach it as we would physics Returning to our earlier example  If you say that the human factorthe difference in personality between Michael Eisner and Bob Igerwas decisive in Disneys acquisition of Pixar  thats a valid observation  But if you try to list all the factors behind the acquisition and hope to replicate this method in the future  youre expecting strict causality and reproducibility This expectation is problematic because acquisitions arent physics  No one can reduce an acquisition to a stepbystep formula and guarantee success if all conditions are met If you recall a small story I later shared about Pixar  youll agree with me  Just before the acquisition was announced  Jobs asked Iger to take a walk with him  Jobs revealed that his cancer had recurred and that his fiveyear survival rate was 50  He told Iger that if he wanted to back out of the deal  now was the time  Iger chose to trust Jobs  and the rest is history  If Iger had decided to delay the acquisition or negotiate a lower price  our entire analysis might not hold  and Disneys current success might not exist Therefore  any business event is influenced by countless factors  As observers and analysts of business  we should certainly strive to summarize  discover patterns  and learn from them  However  we must remember not to treat business like physics  We cant summarize business laws in the same way we do physical laws and expect them to be replicated exactly This is also what makes business fascinating  Its always evolving  and no rule is universally applicable or guaranteed to succeed Once  while chatting with economist Xue Zhaofeng  I mentioned how I envied economists  While economics is also evolving  its theoretical framework is relatively stable  An economist might spend their entire career experiencing only one or two major cognitive shifts  But for those of us studying businessand for you  as a reader of this columnwell experience many cognitive shifts  even revolutions  in our lifetimes because business changes so rapidly  Just ten years ago  the iPhone was introduced  and the entire world transformed  Many business rules from the past no longer apply This is the core message of this column  In the future  whether youre reading my column or listening to successful entrepreneurs or investors dissect business  always remain clearheaded  Understand that these theories can be learned  understood  and even applied  but dont treat them as immutable laws of physics  Otherwise  youll miss the opportunity to truly understand business and grasp its essence Of course  you might wonder  Is studying business
